Timeline

Following March 2020, with schools moving online, I got a lot of time to expand my knowledge and explore the opportunities that the field of Computer Science holds. Graphic Design

One of the first things I took up during the pandemic was Graphic Design, as creating digital art was something I have always wanted to do. I started learning Adobe Illustrator and absolutely loved the entire process. I really encourage people with an interest in art to give it a shot. It was a slow start but over the last few years I have improved a lot and added more onto my skill set by learning Adobe Photoshop as well to help me in making poster designs and other illustrative designs.

I started freelancing for a while but switched my focus to software development and projects soon after. I still make posters and illustrations as a hobby from time to time.

Certifications and Projects

After finally making the decision of pursuing Computer Science in University, I tried to make the most of my "free time" by taking up online courses to explore different fields such as Data Science, Cyber Security, Cloud Computing, Development and more. These courses were mainly taken to give me an idea of what the field holds and what I want to specialize in. Credible or not, I'm happy to have gained a ton of knowledge from these.

After all that theoretical knowledge gained from the Courses and YouTube tutorials, I realized the best way forward now is to put the knowledge into practice and start building my projects. I started working on projects by late 2021. I pushed myself and worked on multiple projects, and learnt a lot about Version Control and GitHub along the way. The pandemic was a terrible thing, but I did find a silver lining as it allowed me to improve a lot as a developer and student.

My First Hackathon

October 2022. I attended my first hackathon, Hack The Valley 7 which was hosted at the University of Toronto Scarborough. Our team decided to work on an incentive-based sustainability tracker for university students. We managed to build a website and a design prototype for our app SustainU. We were learning, building, designing and documenting on the fly as it was the first hackathon for all of my teammates as well.

With over 600+ participants, and people coming from all around Ontario, I did not expect much from myself. Spanning across 3 days of complete sleep deprivation and unhealthy amounts of caffeine, also happened to be my first ever win. SustainU was named the Best Nature Hack. It is one of my proudest achievements till date. All that effort I had put in over the past couple years, had finally paid off.

The MLH Fellowship

The MLH Fellowship is a 12-week internship alternative where fellows collaborate on real world projects for top companies in the field. With an acceptance rate of just over 2%, I never thought that I could get accepted to such a competitive program, but here I am, as a MLH Fellow Graduate of the Summer 2023 batch.

I was tasked with working on LabGraph, a Research Project by Meta that is a framework for rapidly prototyping experimental systems for real-time streaming applications. I was partnered with another fellow to work on a lightweight object detection app for videos stored on a local android device, and logging the detection results in the console. I continue to work on LabGraph in collaboration with a brilliant Research Engineer from Meta, contributing to other open issues.

Currently Working On

I am working in collaboration with the Psychology Department at the University of Toronto Scarborough, on a thesis project revolving around detecting fear stimuli in mice. This research focuses on unraveling the intricate behavioral motifs associated with fear responses using recurrent neural networks.

Additionally, I am also working as a Teaching Assistant at my university, for first-year Computer Science courses. My experience in this role has deepened my appreciation for education and inspired me further to pursue a career in Computer Science. Moreover, I am always trying to find ways to fine-tune my existing projects in terms of efficiency and aesthetics.
